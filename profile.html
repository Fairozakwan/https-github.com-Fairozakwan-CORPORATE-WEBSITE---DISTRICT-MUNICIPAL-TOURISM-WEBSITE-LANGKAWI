Langkawi Municipal Council Tourism City Council (MPLBP)

The service area of Kuah Town was formerly known as the Sanitary Board area. After World War II, during the British administration, the Sanitary Board was converted to Town Board Management starting in 1945.

The MPLBP was known as the Langkawi District Council starting from April 29, 1987. The Langkawi District Council was granted authority as a Local Authority.

The gazetted administrative area of the Langkawi District Council covers an area of 47,848 hectares (478.48 sq km), encompassing 104 islands throughout the Langkawi Archipelago.

The Langkawi Municipal Council Tourism City Council (MPLBP) was gazetted on December 6, 2000, and its declaration was officiated by KDYMM Tuanku Sultan of Kedah Darul Aman on March 24, 2001.

The MPLBP has received ISO Version 9001:2008 certification, and its quality management has been improved through ISO version 9001:2015 certification.

The scope of MPLBP's work includes physical, economic, and social planning towards the stability of urban development in Langkawi and providing efficient and responsible municipal services for urban planning and development.

The MPLBP administration is always responsible for carrying out the tasks entrusted efficiently and effectively and always strives to provide and implement quality services for the excellence, satisfaction, and comfort of residents and taxpayers.

MPLBP carries out each of its functions using the Local Government Act 1976 (Act 171) and is able to carry out activities and enforce policies and regulations related to Local Authority Affairs.
